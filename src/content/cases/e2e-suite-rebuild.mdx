## The problem

The team had stopped trusting their test suite. 35% of tests failed on any given CI run — not because of bugs, but because of race conditions, shared state, and brittle selectors. The response was to add retries. Three retries per test. The suite took 40 minutes.

When the CI dashboard shows red so often that green becomes surprising, the suite has failed its job.

## The approach

**Phase 1: Audit.** Categorised every failing test: race condition, shared state, broken selector, or legitimate bug. 80% of failures were automation problems, not product bugs.

**Phase 2: Redesign.** Replaced the flat test structure with a proper POM. Created shared fixtures for auth, test data creation, and cleanup. Rewrote selectors to use `getByRole`, `getByLabel`, and `data-testid` attributes.

**Phase 3: Rebuild.** Migrated incrementally — Playwright running in parallel with Cypress, both in CI, until the new suite matched coverage.

**Phase 4: Handoff.** Runbook, live walkthrough, first maintenance PR written by the client team.

## The outcome

Zero flake rate on 450 tests across 6 weeks of production CI runs. Run time dropped from 40 minutes to 13 minutes through parallelisation. The team's first response to a CI failure is now "what broke?" — not "probably flaky, retry it."
